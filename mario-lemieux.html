Mario Lemieux - Pittsburgh Penguins

Bio

Mario Lemieux is a Pittsburgh sports legend who saved the Penguins both on and off the ice. Drafted first overall in 1984, he became one of the NHL’s most dominant scorers, leading Pittsburgh to back-to-back Stanley Cups in 1991 and 1992. Despite battling serious health issues throughout his career, “Super Mario” won three MVP awards, two scoring titles, and later returned as owner of the Penguins, ensuring the team’s long-term success in Pittsburgh.
